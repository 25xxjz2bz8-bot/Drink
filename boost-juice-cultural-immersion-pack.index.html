Both converge on transparency, quality, and intentionality — but through different entry points.

Both generations are driving the mocktail premiumisation wave, but for different reasons: Gen Z for social aesthetics, Millennials for mindful health.

Particularly potent for summer launches targeting older Gen Z (18–24) who want adventure without alcohol.

For Boost Juice, the juice production process generates significant fruit pulp — positioning a upcycled pulp-forward drink format could be a genuine category first.

Strategic read — Future signals
The 9–12 month NPD window is where the most valuable white space sits. Matcha, PSL and espresso martini are all at or past peak — building for those now means arriving late. The genuinely differentiated opportunity for Boost Juice is at the intersection of its heritage (fresh fruit, functional blending) and the incoming waves: gut-brain drinks, adaptogen mood stacking, fibre-forward formats, and the "newstalgia" repositioning of tropical classics. These aren't trend chases — they're the extension of consumer behaviour trajectories already proven in adjacent categories, now heading to Boost Juice's natural home territory.
